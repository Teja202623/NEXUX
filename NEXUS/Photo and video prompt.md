# ORIGINAL REFERENCE MATERIAL

## 1. Image Prompts (Nano Banana)

  Structure: [Action] + [Subject] + [Context] + [Details]

  Example from video:
  "Man holding prime product in one hand holding the American flag
  in the other while sitting on a jet ski"

  Technique:
  - Specific positioning - "in one hand...in the other" (not just "holding")
  - Concrete scenario - "sitting on a jet ski" (not generic)
  - Clear subject - prime product is the focal point
  - Visual composition - creates natural framing

  Another example:
  "Husky eating from a metal bowl of dog food at the edge of
  Half-Dome in Yosemite. Scenic view in the background."

  Techniques here:
  - Specific breed/object - "husky" (not just "dog")
  - Detail about material - "metal bowl" (adds realism)
  - Geographic specificity - "Half-Dome in Yosemite" (creates mood)
  - Layering - foreground action + background context

  2. Text Overlay Prompt (Critical for Ad Quality)

  Structure: "[Fill instruction] + [Text content] + [Styling]"

  From the automation:
  "Fill the entire canvas with [IMAGE_IDEA]. Put the text 
  [TAGLINE] centered between the subject at the top and a 
  high contrast color Instagram story font."

  Techniques:
  - Canvas instruction - "fill the entire canvas" (ensures coverage)
  - Text positioning - "centered between the subject at the top" (specific
  placement)
  - Contrast requirement - "high contrast color" (readability for ads)
  - Format specification - "Instagram story font" (platform-specific)

  3. Video Prompts (Veo 3)

  Key principle: KEEP IT EXTREMELY SIMPLE

  Bad (too complex): ❌
  "Dog happily eats food while looking at camera,
  with sunset in background, slow motion"

  Good (simple): ✅
  "Dog happily eats food"

  or

  "Man says nothing is better than drinking a prime on 4th of July"

  Why simple works:
  - Video AI makes fewer mistakes with simple concepts
  - Complex instructions often result in wrong outputs
  - Simple = better control over the final product

  4. Product Context Layer

  The creator uses a 3-tier input system:

  1. Product Info (from Google Sheets)
    - Object/Product name
    - Description/Context paragraph
    - Product image link
  2. AI-Generated Taglines (from ChatGPT)
    - Short, punchy text for the overlay
    - Generated in bulk from product description
  3. Manual Creative Briefs (Human creative judgment)
    - Image idea
    - Video idea
    - Kept simple and visual

  Key insight: The creator notes that humans are better at image ideas than AI.
  Manual creative input > AI-generated creative.

  5. Prompt Template (from ChatGPT)

  The creator uses a ChatGPT prompt to generate 10 image/video idea variations:

  Process:
  1. Upload product image to ChatGPT
  2. Provide product description
  3. Get 10 image idea suggestions
  4. Get 10 video idea suggestions
  5. Manually filter and refine

  Important: Creator manually reviews and refines before using (AI suggestions
  often too complex for videos).

  6. Ad-Specific Prompting

  For ad purposes, the creator emphasizes:

## PROMPTING TIPS & TRICKS

### DO's:
✅ Use specific location names (Denver, not "a city")
✅ Include specific professional details (brand uniform, tool type)
✅ Reference actual emotions (confident, satisfied, relieved)
✅ Keep video prompts to 1 action, 1 location, 1 subject
✅ Use high-contrast colors for text overlays
✅ Test prompts with a few before bulk creation
✅ Save successful prompts for future use

### DON'Ts:
❌ Don't use generic descriptions ("a plumber fixing pipes")
❌ Don't add multiple actions to video prompts
❌ Don't request special effects (slow-mo, filters, etc.)
❌ Don't use light/low-contrast text on light backgrounds
❌ Don't assume AI knows your city/business specifics
❌ Don't skip manual review of AI-generated ideas
❌ Don't create more than 2-3 variations per business initially
